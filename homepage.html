Machine Learning

LLM for STEM education

Collaborated with Dr. Xiaoming Zhai and Dr. Latif on adapting large language models (LLMs) for high school physics education. Following a systematic review of Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITS) and Robotic Tutoring Systems (RTS) and their use of LLMs for delivering instruction, our project focuses on fine-tuning models to teach and respond to physics questions at the high school level. Our approach leverages prompt engineering, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and low-rank adaptation (LoRA) to enhance accuracy, accessibility, and pedagogical effectiveness.

Genetic Cars

Developed a genetic evolution experiment in the Unity game engine to simulate the optimization of off-road vehicles. Each car was represented by a set of genes, including body type (truck, SUV, sedan, etc) and continuous properties such as wheel size, suspension travel, and engine horsepower. An initial population of cars was randomly generated and tasked with traversing challenging terrain. Vehicles that traveled farther were more likely to pass their genes to the next generation, leading to the emergence of increasingly capable off-road designs over time.

Infect, Detect, Protect

Implemented Unity's ML-Agents framework to train AI agents using reinforcement learning and imitation learning to target cancerous cells while avoiding healthy ones. I programmed “white knight” agents, modeled after white blood cells, to identify cancerous cells based on their behaviors and genetic traits. By combining reinforcement learning with imitation learning, the agents achieved stronger, more reliable performance by swiftly detecting and neutralizing cancerous cells before they could multiply.

Dodgeball Agents

Developed a dodgeball simulation using finite state machines (FSM) to control agent behaviors such as offense, defense, and teammate retrieval. This approach enabled agents to exhibit adaptive intelligence, dynamically adjusting strategies to maximize their chances of winning. In addition, the projectile motion of the dodgeball was modeled with physics, allowing agents to predict opponents' future positions and optimize throwing accuracy.

Fuzzy Racing

Developed a 3D racing simulation in Unity incorporating an AI-controlled vehicle governed by a fuzzy logic controller. The system uses fuzzy inference to dynamically modulate steering, throttle, and braking in response to continuous input variables including velocity, track boundary proximity, and turn curvature. This approach enables the AI to produce adaptive, human-like driving behaviors, achieving smoother trajectories and more realistic handling compared to traditional rule-based or pathfinding methods.
